Our History

Walchand College of Engineering, Sangli was established in 1947 by the Maharashtra Tatwagyan Education Society (MTE Society) at the initiative of the late Seth Walchand Hirachand, a visionary industrialist who believed that quality technical education was the cornerstone of India's development.

Starting with just two branches — Civil and Mechanical Engineering — the college has grown into a full-spectrum engineering institution offering undergraduate, postgraduate, diploma, and doctoral programmes across 11 departments.

1947
Founding of WCE
Established by MTE Society on the initiative of Seth Walchand Hirachand. Commenced with Civil and Mechanical Engineering branches under Bombay University affiliation.
1962
Expansion of Programmes
Added Electrical and Electronics Engineering, responding to India's post-independence industrialisation push. Student strength doubled within a decade.
1974
Affiliation to Shivaji University
Following the establishment of Shivaji University in Kolhapur, WCE became affiliated, strengthening its regional academic leadership.
1994
Computer Science Department
Launched the Department of Computer Science and Engineering to meet the emerging demand of India's software revolution. One of the first engineering colleges in the region to do so.
2006
NBA Accreditation
Multiple departments received NBA (National Board of Accreditation) accreditation, recognising WCE's academic standards at the national level.
2012
Autonomous Status Granted
WCE was granted Autonomous Institute status by UGC and Shivaji University, enabling greater flexibility in curriculum design, examinations, and academic governance.
2015
NAAC Accreditation
The college received NAAC accreditation, affirming excellence across teaching-learning, research, infrastructure, and institutional values.
2022
AI, ML & Robotics Departments
Launched new-age departments — Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning and Robotics & Automation — to prepare students for the Fourth Industrial Revolution.
2024
Platinum Jubilee Celebrations
WCE celebrated 75+ years of engineering education with alumni meets, symposia, and launch of new research initiatives. Over 30,000 alumni across the globe participated in centenary events.
